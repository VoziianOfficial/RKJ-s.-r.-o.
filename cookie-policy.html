1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files that websites may place on your device when you visit a page. Similar technologies, such as local storage, may also be used to save simple website preferences.

Cookies and local storage can help websites remember choices, support security, understand website performance, and improve usability.

2. How This Website May Use Cookies

The website for RKJ s. r. o. may use cookies or local storage for limited website-related purposes.

  • Remembering your cookie banner choice
  • Supporting core website functionality
  • Improving website performance and usability
  • Understanding general visitor interaction patterns
  • Helping maintain security and prevent misuse

3. Essential Cookies and Storage

Essential cookies or local storage may be needed for basic website functionality. For example, when you click Accept or Decline in the policy banner, the website stores that choice so the banner does not keep appearing on every page.

The suggested local storage key for this website is rkjPolicyChoice. This stores only the selected preference value, such as accepted or declined.

4. Analytics and Performance Cookies

The website may use basic analytics-style or performance-related tools to understand how visitors use pages and how the website performs. This information can help improve content, layout, speed, and user experience.

Analytics-style data may include general information such as page views, browser type, device type, approximate interaction patterns, and technical performance signals.

Marketing performance, campaign outcomes, rankings, leads, revenue, and advertising results are not guaranteed by the use of analytics or any website technology.

5. Preference Cookies

Preference cookies or local storage may be used to remember choices you make on the website. The main preference stored by this website is your cookie banner decision.

If you clear browser storage, use a different browser, or visit from another device, the website may ask you to make the choice again.

6. Cookie Banner Behavior

The cookie and policy banner appears when no previous Accept or Decline choice has been stored. It does not close automatically and should only close after you click Accept or Decline.

  • Accept saves your acceptance choice in local storage.
  • Decline saves your decline choice in local storage.
  • The banner should remain visible until one of these choices is selected.
  • Once a choice is stored, the banner should not appear again unless browser storage is cleared.

7. Managing Cookies in Your Browser

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block cookies, delete cookies, clear local storage, or receive alerts before cookies are stored.

Browser controls are usually available in the privacy, security, or site settings area of your browser. If you disable some storage features, parts of the website may not remember your preferences.
